History of Hong Kong

Modern-day Hong Kong is best known for its sprawl of skyscrapers, a bustling financial hub off the southern coast of mainland China and a regional conduit for trade.

But the territory was once a quiet backwater of rural hamlets and fishing communities, where mountainous terrain dominated sparse human settlement.

Hong Kong was part of the British empire until 1997, when the lease on the New Territories expired and the entire city was handed back to China.

Demographic Information

The current population of Hong Kong in 2021 is 7,552,810, a 0.74% increase from 2020.

The population of Hong Kong in 2020 was 7,496,981, a 0.82% increase from 2019.

The population of Hong Kong in 2019 was 7,436,154, a 0.87% increase from 2018.

The population of Hong Kong in 2018 was 7,371,730, a 0.9% increase from 2017.

The Attractiveness of Hong Kong

Why Hong Kong is the perfect environment for business:

  • A strategic gateway to growing Mainland China markets
  • A regional financial and logistics hub for expansion across Asia
  • A stable, highly-efficient, business-friendly cosmopolitan
  • Only annual tax and company filing are required
  • Business nature and location of operation are not restricted
  • Online registration of Hong Kong Limited Company is allowed(no physical presence in Hong Kong of directors and shareholders is required when the registration is handled by a company service provider in Hong Kong, e.g. AsiaBC)
  • Requirement of local office address and company secretary is easy to follow, hire a company service provider to provide them with your company
